Spike Prime FLL Challenges

Apply the helpers from master.py in real tasks. These challenges are designed to take you from basic motion tests to full FLL-style missions.

Lessons index: Spike Prime FLL Lessons · Repo: github.com/agent6/SpikePrime2025FLL

Challenge Group C1 – Straight-Line Accuracy

Practice using drive_forward and drive_backward to move accurately along a straight line, then return to base. Focus on distance and heading control using encoders and yaw.

  • C1.1 – Hit the Line
    Drive straight from base and stop on a marked line using drive_forward.

Challenge Group C2 – Turning & Alignment

Use pivot and spin helpers to control robot orientation precisely. Practice making clean 90° turns, in‑place spins, and aligning the robot to field lines or walls.

  • C2.1 – Square Path with Pivots
    Use drive_forward and pivot_*() helpers to drive a four-sided square and end aligned near the start.

Challenge Group C3 – Attachments & Models

Practice using left and right attachments to interact with models. Focus on lifting, tapping, and returning mechanisms to a safe home position using encoder degrees and MotorIsStopped.

  • C3.1 – Tap the Target
    Use left_attachment_*() or right_attachment_*() to gently tap a model and return to a safe home position.

Challenge Group C4 – Combined Motion (Drive + Attach)

Combine driving and attachments using async helpers. Practice raising or lowering mechanisms while the robot is moving, and coordinating timing so actions finish at the right moment.

  • C4.1 – Drive While Lifting
    Drive using async_drive_forward while lifting an attachment in parallel with async helpers.

Challenge Group C5 – Mission Builder & Optimization

Design full FLL-style missions using the helpers in master.py, then optimize them for speed and reliability. Focus on chaining movements, attachments, and selector-driven mission slots.

  • C5.1 – Build a Full Model Mission
    Turn a test mission into a real model run, then tune it for reliability and speed.

Challenge Group C6 – Robustness & Recovery

Make missions more resilient to variation and mistakes. Focus on using feedback (encoders, MotorIsStopped, and mission structure) to recover from small errors and keep the robot in a known, safe state between runs.

  • C6.1 – Robust Re-home & Recovery
    Build a recovery mission that re-homes attachments using MotorIsStopped and ends in a known safe state.
