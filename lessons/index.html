Spike Prime FLL Lessons

2025 FLL Spike Prime Python v3 training sequence. Start with Python basics, then progress into robot motion, attachments, async behavior, and full missions using master.py.

Repo: github.com/agent6/SpikePrime2025FLL · Overview: LEGO SPIKE Prime with Python Lessons

Phase 1 – Python Basics (Online IDE)

Learn core Python concepts in a browser (variables, control flow, loops, data structures) using the online playground. No robot required – focus on the language first.

  • Lesson 1 – Python Foundations
    Variables, basic types, expressions, and print formatting.
  • Lesson 2 – Control Flow
    If/elif/else, comparisons, logical operators, and simple decisions.
  • Lesson 3 – Loops and Functions
    While/for loops, defining functions, and a text-only robot project.
  • Lesson 4 – Lists and Dictionaries (Stretch)
    Lists for sequences and dictionaries for named settings.

Phase 2 – Spike Hardware & Runtime

Move into the Spike App IDE. Explore the hub, ports, motors, sensors, and the async runloop model used on the Spike Prime hub.

  • Lesson 5 – Spike Hardware & Ports
    Tour of the hub, ports, motors, sensors, and our robot wiring.
  • Lesson 6 – Motors, Encoders, and Motion Sensor
    Basic motor commands, encoders, and using yaw from the motion sensor.
  • Lesson 7 – Async and runloop
    What async/await mean on the hub, and how to wait for events.
  • Lesson 16 – Controlling Motion with Tilt
    Using motion sensor gestures and tilt with if‑conditions to trigger images and sounds.
  • Lesson 17 – Importing Libraries
    Why we import libraries at the top of a Python program to connect code and SPIKE hardware.
  • Lesson 18 – Making Moves with Motors
    Intro to single and dual motor control, awaitables, and a creative motor dance party.
  • Lesson 19 – Start Sensing
    Using the force sensor with runloop.until to start, stop, and control motors and messages.
  • Lesson 20 – New Moves with Motors
    Programming motors with degrees and absolute positions for precise, repeatable movement.

Phase 3 – Core Robot Movement & Attachments

Use master.py helpers for straight driving, pivots, spins, and attachments. Learn how proportional correction, encoders, and yaw give accurate, repeatable motion.

  • Lesson 8 – Straight Driving
    Using drive helpers, yaw correction, and field tuning for distance.
  • Lesson 9 – Pivot Turns
    Inside/outside pivots and using yaw-based angles for precise turns.
  • Lesson 10 – Spin Turns
    In-place spin turns and when to choose spins vs pivots.
  • Lesson 11 – Attachments
    Driving left/right attachments using encoder degrees.
  • Lesson 12 – Async Motion Helpers
    Async driving and attachments for parallel motion.
  • Lesson 13 – Detecting Motor Stop
    Using encoder feedback and MotorIsStopped to wait for mechanisms.
  • Lesson 14 – Mission Selector UX
    How the mission selector loop and button UX work.
  • Lesson 15 – Mission Functions
    Designing full missions in mission_0–mission_9.

Per-Helper Lessons – Driving & Turning

  • Lesson DF – drive_forward
    Deep dive on forward driving and tuning kP/speeds.
  • Lesson DB – drive_backward
    Reverse driving, straightness, and safe backing up.
  • Lesson PLO – pivot_left_outside
    Turning left around the left wheel using the right wheel forward.
  • Lesson PLI – pivot_left_inside
    Left turns using the inside wheel backing up.
  • Lesson PRO – pivot_right_outside
    Turning right around the right wheel using the left wheel forward.
  • Lesson PRI – pivot_right_inside
    Right turns using the inside wheel backing up.
  • Lesson SL – spin_left
    In-place left spins and tuning angles.
  • Lesson SR – spin_right
    In-place right spins and tuning angles.

Per-Helper Lessons – Attachments

  • Lesson LAU – left_attachment_up
    Moving the left attachment upward using degrees and speed.
  • Lesson LAD – left_attachment_down
    Returning the left attachment to its home position.
  • Lesson RAU – right_attachment_up
    Raising or rotating the right-side attachment.
  • Lesson RAD – right_attachment_down
    Lowering the right-side attachment back to home.

Per-Helper Lessons – Async Helpers

  • Lesson ADF – async_drive_forward
    Forward driving that can run in parallel with other async tasks.
  • Lesson ADB – async_drive_backward
    Backward driving designed for parallel async behavior.
  • Lesson ALAU – async_left_attachment_up
    Raising the left attachment while other async actions run.
  • Lesson ALAD – async_left_attachment_down
    Lowering the left attachment in parallel with motion.
  • Lesson ARAU – async_right_attachment_up
    Raising the right attachment while driving or turning.
  • Lesson ARAD – async_right_attachment_down
    Lowering the right attachment in parallel with other tasks.

Per-Helper Lessons – Control Helpers & Missions

  • Lesson MIS – MotorIsStopped
    Checking encoder movement to see when a motor has stopped.
  • Lesson SEL – mission_selector
    The mission selection menu loop and UI logic.
  • Lesson Mx – mission_0–mission_9
    How to turn mission slots into full FLL runs.

Challenges – Apply What You’ve Learned

Put the helpers from master.py into practice with focused challenges: straight-line accuracy, turning and alignment, attachments, async motion, mission building, and robustness.

  • Challenges Index
    Start with C1.1 – Hit the Line to tune drive_forward, then try C2–C6 for pivots and spins, attachments, async drive+attach motion, full missions, and robustness and recovery.
